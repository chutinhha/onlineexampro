The Oman Debate  
     
     
     
 

Date : 2 November 2011
Venue: Al Bustan Palace, Ritz Carlton Hotel

Session 1: Oman: Development versus stability?

The Arab Spring has thrown up gaps in the development process in various countries across the region forcing governments to rethink about their policies and priorities. Oman too has been forced to go back to the drawing board to relook at its future course of action. The measures taken to address various issues has led to various questions/concerns in its trail. The Oman Debate will raises a few of these :

  1. Job creation : Knee jerk reaction or sustainable effort?
    The government announced the creation of 50,000 jobs and has pressured the private sector to follow suit in creating jobs. Is creating 50,000 jobs in one year the solution to the unemployment problem or are there structural issues like changes in the education system, making people employable, training etc that need to be looked at.
     

  2. Raising salaries, sops and benefits : Can the corporate sector foot the bill?
    The government has raised the minimum salary and social security benefits for Omanis saddling the private sector with a huge wage bill. At a time when the economy was recovering from the global crisis, these have come as a double whammy. The government has set Omanisation targets for various sectors but the private sector is struggling to find suitable/employable Omanis to fill those positions. Is the private sector being made to foot the bill for the government’s failings or has the corporate sector failed to do its part in the development process?
     

  3. Economic ramifications of these measures/protests: The protests have impacted the cost of doing business in Oman, budgetary outflow, the GDP, FDI inflows and credit rating of the country. Will this be a momentary shock or will it leave a long term impact?
     

  4. Oman : A defining moment. Is this a watershed moment in Oman’s socio-economic history where everything that was held as sacred needs to be questioned and redefined?
     

Session 2: At the crossroads

A quick fire round in which three panelists will be posed with a list of questions regarding the current business environment, topics of concerns and the state of the economy like

  1. Is the current business environment conducive to growth?

  2. Are the labour laws in Oman skewed in favour of employees?

  3. Are the current Omanisation quotas unrealistic?

  4. Are companies shirking their responsibility using various excuses when it comes to Omanisation?

  5. Will the recent protests have a negative impact on FDI and the image of Oman?

  6. Have the protests led to more transparency and accountability in the public sector?

  7. Does the Omani Education system need a major revamp?

  8. Is there a need to enhance awareness of work ethic amongst job aspirants in Oman?

  9. Is everyone paying lip service to SME development?

  10. Is private public partnership still a far cry from what it should be?

    The interactive session will give the audience to participate in the deliberations and indicate their stand on various questions.
