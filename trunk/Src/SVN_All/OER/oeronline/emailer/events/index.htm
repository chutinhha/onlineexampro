New Government - New Policies

Dear Colleague,

We are very pleased to bring to your attention the Oman Business and Investment Summit 2011, firmly scheduled for 28/29 March next, at the Grand Hyatt in Muscat. This will be the first major International Conference showcasing the New Government and its policies. Stability, business as usual and investor friendly environment are their key policies and reflect the statement below by the US Secretary of State.

The Sultanate of Oman has made more progress than any country in the world in the past 40 years, which has laid the stability at home and peace with its neighbors. It must be for other countries to learn how to become the Sultanate in this short period of time a unique model of development in the Arab States, and knowledge of the foundations that have made them to become a model for development in the Middle East.

Hillary Clinton, US Secretary of State, during an Official visit to Oman in January 2011.

Key Topics include:

  • Oman's Vision 2020 master plan still in place

  • Substantial economic diversification and modernization under a New Government

  • Government committed to promoting FDIs and trade

  • Strong and stable banking system backing growth-oriented business

  • Knowledge-based IT programmes - towards a digital economy

  • Promising long-term prospect for private equity

  • Large infrastructure projects development

  • Renewables - towards hydro power and solar energy shift

  • Industrial expansion and opportunities in the free zone areas

  • The tourism and real estate sectors show confidence

These are only some of the key selling points for Oman, having already attracted many new foreign investors into the country.

The Summit will enable you and your colleagues to see for yourselves, explore the business opportunities and network with both senior representatives from Europe, GCC alongside the Omani business community and other local decision-makers.
