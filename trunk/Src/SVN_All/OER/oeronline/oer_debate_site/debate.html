Debate - The Way Ahead – Road to Vision 2020  
     
     
   
     
     
 

Date: 25th October 2010
Venue: Capital Market Authority Auditorium
Time: 3.30pm

The OER Debate 2010 is the second edition of the most acclaimed business forum in the Sultanate and involves corporate leaders and policy makers in an in-depth discussion on the current state of the Omani economy.

Topics to be discussed at the OER Debate 2010:

 Are we out of the woods?
As the global financial crisis fades away it’s time to take stock of the way ahead. While most people feel that things have improved, fears of a double dip (recession) still persist. Unemployment in the US is running at record levels; consumer spending and demand are still weak. In Oman, real estate, automotive and retail spending has not picked up putting a question mark on growth. Where do you stand?

 Is this the new normal?
The frenzied growth rates witnessed between 2007-2008, have raised people’s expectations to such an extent, that by comparison growth rates (achieved and projected) look meager. The Sultanate has pursued a steady economic policy and people need to get used to such growth rates as the new normal. Have our expectations become unjustified?

Are the government and the private sector working at cross purposes?
The business community feels that the speed of decision making by the government is tardy, the methodology being followed by the government’s Omanisation policy is being questioned by the private sector. The government on its part feels that the businesses do not want to hire
Omanis on their own, the private sector is used to free lunches and making money easily, without shouldering their social responsibilities? Are the government and the private sector working at cross purposes and is there a need for better public private partnership?

Is enough being done to achieve Vision 2020?
The vision 2020 has laid out various pillars of development like IT, Business, Human Resource Development, Industry and Tourism. While the goals have been listed out there are question marks on the progress made towards them? The service levels in critical areas like health and education are not up to the mark. How do you go ahead from here and are the goals of 2020 achievable?

The OER Debate 2010 is in association with Capital Market Authority and our chief guests will be H.E. Maqbool Bin Ali Sultan, Minister for Commerce and Industry, and H.E. Yahya Al Jabri, Executive President, Capital Market Authority.
