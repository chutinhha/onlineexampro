DOSSIER PROFILE

ABOUT DOSSIER CONSTRUCTION MAGAZINE

Dossier-Construction is Oman’s first and only Business to Business (B2B) magazine on the sector for the last seven years. Published by United Media Services (UMS), known for Oman Economic Review magazine, Dossier-Construction targets the who’s who of the industry: procurement and purchasing heads, top officials in the ministries, senior-to-mid level managers in the construction firms, independent consultants and building material suppliers, industry bodies and visitors/participants in relevant trade forums and more. An authoritative source of information, Dossier-Construction is a strategic partner with all the major industry trade shows in Muscat.









EVENT CONCEPT



DOSSIER CONSTRUCTION AWARDS & SUMMIT 2013

UMS is recognized for premier corporate events such as OMAN DEBATE hosted by Tim Sebastian, OMAN FORUM hosted by Yusuf Al Hooti, OER TOP 20 AWARDS, AIWA AWARDS and OER CEO GOLF, etc. After the unprecedented success of DOSSIER-CONSTRUCTION print magazine, UMS takes the opportunity to connect with the industry stakeholders through DOSSIER CONSTRUCTION AWARDS & SUMMIT 2013 -- an exclusive construction, real estate and infrastructure industry event.

The objective is to felicitate industry performers, individuals, best practices, technology and project excellence through an awards ceremony; and drive the exchange of knowledge and best practices within the industry through an intellectual forum in the form of a Summit.

The one day event will include a half-day long Summit followed by a special award ceremony in the evening felicitating the best of the best within the construction, infrastructure, real estate, interiors and building materials’ industries.

DETAILED PROGRAMME


BRIEF SCHEDULE

8.30 AM – 1.00 PM SUMMIT
7.30 – 9.00 PM AWARDS CEREMONY

THE SUMMIT

DOSSIER CONSTRUCTION AWARDS & SUMMIT 2013’s first half session dedicated to the Summit aims to bring about impactful and compelling educational sessions from senior-level international and local leaders of consulting, construction and real estate companies who bring to the forefront challenging issues and new insights into the business opportunities in today’s construction market.

This industry's best and experienced minds cover diverse topics, such as: Workforce & labour management, project finance, tenders, infrastructure development, best practices, local talent cultivation and more. It offers a blend of topics—some on core matters that are covered from different angles and perspectives, plus a variety of new topics covering current issues, trends, and problems.

SUMMIT SCHEDULE

8.30 – 9.00 AM Registration
9.00 – 9.05 AM Inauguration & Key Note Address by the Chief Guest
9.05 – 9.20 AM Talk by HE Sultan bin Salim Al-Habsi, Secretary General of the Supreme Council for Planning,
  on the vision and focus areas for the national development plan

9.20 – 9.35 AM Talk by HE Dr. Rasheed bin Al Safi Al Huraibi, Chairman of Oman Tender Board, on efforts
  undertaken to improve the tendering process and the issues that needs to be addressed in the coming days

9.35 – 9.50 AM Presentation by a senior official from the Ministry of Transport & Communications on ‘Oman’s Transport
  Infrastructure Developments and Key Projects in the Pipeline’

9.50 – 10.05 AM Presentation by HE Maitha Al Mahrouqi, Undersecretary, Ministry of Tourism
  on ‘Charting Oman’s Tourism Infrastructure’
10.05 – 10.20 Talk by an international senior project engineer on his/her experience of working on a Mega Structure
10.20 – 11.00 Networking Break and Refreshments
11.00 AM – 12 NOON Panel Discussion on:

ADDRESSING THE OMANI CONSTRUCTION INDUSTRY’S KEY CHALLENGES
Comprehensive panel discussion about the current challenges facing the construction industry in Oman including labour issues, market competition, building competitiveness, contracting terms, payment delays, incompetency, role of the Government etc.
The Panelists will include head honchos of top construction firms and senior representatives of the Ministry of Manpower and Ministry of Commerce & Industry.

12 NOON – 1.00 PM Panel Discussion on:

BUILDING SMART CITIES
Going beyond buildings and networks – strategies for smart urban planning. Is Oman ready for the future? The Panelists will include a mix of experts with technical known-how as well as members who can provide a bird’s eye view on how Oman could build smarter cities as part of its urban development planning.
