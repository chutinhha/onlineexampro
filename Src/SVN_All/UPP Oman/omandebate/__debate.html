Oman Debate 2014

Date: Apirl, 2014
Venue: Majan Ballroom, Al Bustan Palace, A Ritz Carlton Hotel 
Time: 9.00-12.30 pm 

Topic: In-Country Value Oman – The National Priority
 

  1. BACKGROUND NOTE ON ICV:
    The promotion of local products & services and development & deployment of Omani human resources has been a priority area for the Government and the private sector for quite some time. The oil and gas sector in Oman has moved the agenda to a high ground in the form of a well-researched, comprehensively defined and structured programme called ‘IN-COUNTRY VALUE DEVELOPMENT – BLUEPRINT STRATEGY’. ICV is the total spend retained in-country that can benefit business development, contribute to human capability development and stimulate productivity in the Omani economy. In short, products made and services provided by skilled Omanis. The strategy entails ICV opportunities of $64 billion in the period 2013-2020 encompassing business prospects for the Omani/Oman based companies and employment of Omanis in the activities related to the oil & gas sector.
     

  2. POINTS TO BE DISCUSSED:
    Oman Debate 2014 will discuss the ICV Blueprint Strategy in the overall national economic and social development context as per the following points:

    • Briefly review the ICV Blueprint strategy and a detailed debate on the associated challenges and opportunities.
    • The multiplier effect of the strategy on the non-oil & gas industries as it has a direct bearing on the services (banking, finance, telecom, IT, logistics etc.) and manufacturing sectors, etc.
    • Boon to the small and medium enterprises (SMEs)
    • The learnings from the strategy which could be applied in other key sectors such as tourism, banking, IT, telecom, manufacturing, retail and logistics.
    • Deliberate on the need and feasibility aspect for making a similar ICV policy for the entire national economy and the related incentive and monitoring mechanism.
    • Reflect upon its impact on foreign direct investment (FDI).
    • Assess if it would really give the desired boost to the home grown private sector companies, if the strategy is rolled-out across sectors at the national level.
