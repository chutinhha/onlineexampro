The Oman Debate  
     
     
     
 

Oman Debate 2012

Date: 3rd December, 2012
Venue: Oman Auditorium, Al Bustan Palace, A Ritz Carlton Hotel
Time: 9.00-12.30 pm

Moderator: Tim Sebastian

Chief guest and keynote speaker: HE Ali bin Masoud Al Sunaidi, Minister of Commerce and Industry

Topic: Is Oman Future Ready?
 

  1. Job creation:
    The government has been promoting manufacturing industries in various industrial estates like Sohar, Salalah and Rusayl. This coupled with the word class facilities coming up in Duqm will give a fillip to infrastructure development and create thousands of jobs in future. Efforts are also being made to promote SME’s and they will be another big driver for job creation. Overall, these developments look very promising for the Sultanate’s economy.
     

  2. Doing business in Oman:
    The various reforms taken by the government in the wake of the labour unrest last year in January-February 2011 diffused a volatile situation and ensured that Oman negotiated the fallout of such unrest better than other countries in the region. But measures like increasing minimum wages, creating new jobs and introduction of a shorter working week is creating obstacles for businesses. According to an ILO report 70 per cent of companies in Oman believe that Oman’s regulatory environment makes it difficult for small businesses to compete with large businesses, while 40 per cent of firms feel that there is too much of red tape. The decision making process in Oman is slow and there is little interdepartmental cooperation (e.g. Haya water a government own agency requires 9 permits for building something near its office, and the proposal has been in the pipeline for 6 months). Rules are regulations are open to interpretation and change from person to person.
     

  3. Measures needed to bridge the gap between aims/intentions and ground realities
    What needs to be done to see that the correct policies get formulated and that they are executed to meet the intended goals. A look at sunrise sectors like Tourism, Infrastructure, Education, IT etc and how they can contribute to economic development.
