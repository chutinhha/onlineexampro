Oman Debate 2014

Prominent business leaders and In-Country Value (ICV) experts called for more concerted efforts to apply Oman’s ICV strategy in non-oil & gas sectors in the country, at Oman Debate 2014. Anchored by renowned television host Riz Khan, the fifth edition of the annual national forum witnessed no-holds-barred discussions and animated exchanges on how the ICV model can help in creating value, generating jobs for Omanis, promoting Small and Medium Enterprises and diversifying the base of the economy. The Debate concluded that enhancing the skillset of the local workforce, promoting entrepreneurship and SMEs, inculcating work ethics, bringing more transparency and accountability and achieving a more robust private-public partnership are the key areas to meet an ambitious ICV strategy.

Organised by OER in association with Capital Marek Authority, under the patronage of HH Sayyid Tarik Al Said, Editor-in-Chief, OER, and HE Abdullah al Salmi, Executive President, Capital Market Authority, the fifth edition of Oman Debate featured an august panel of speakers from the government, and the private sector to represent a cross section of opinions. Divided into two sessions titled In Country Value Oman: Identifying priorities and Is - In Country Value Oman: Creating a sustainable model, the Debate identified potential opportunities for enterprises and Omanis as a result of the $64 billion of retained earnings during the period from 2013-2020.

Thought provoking

Setting the tone for a day of thought-provoking debates and deliberations, Engineer Simbi K. Wabote, Contracting and Procurement Global Local Content Manager, Shell EP International, did a presentation on In-Country Value, identifying the potential areas of generating local values globally and highlighting the importance of replicating them in Oman.

Wael Al Lawati opined that ICV demands a sense of responsibility for maximising local content in the businesses. He added that in a sector like tourism which requires more authenticity and local association ICV is instrumental in giving a local flavour to the services. Dr Amer Al Rawas called for passing on the successful experience of local talent development in sectors like oli& gas, banking and telecom to other sectors. He added that visibility and transparency are the two important factors in bringing about the desired goals. Husam Al Jahdhami stressed the importance of technology, human resources development and legislation in generating more local content. According to Gert Hoefman, CEO, Oman Cables Industries, bringing about more innovation is essential to take ICV forward in Oman’s manufacturing sector.

Raphael Parambi opined that SME development is crucial to take ICV concept to other sectors. He said there needs to be more incentives for private sectors to implement Omanisation. He added that although private sector needs to be more accountable in training the employees, inculcating work ethics among employees is a big challenge.

Talking in the second panel discussion Irshad Al Lawati explained the comprehensive ICV strategy for Oman’s oil and gas sector adding that the strategy is still in its early stage of execution and it is too early to talk about the results. Referring to the difficulties in applying the strategy in other sectors, he opined that although good training centers are available in the country there is a shortage in the number of programmes that can meet the requirements.

Said Al Sarhani said that it will take time to celebrate the success of ICV. “The journey is about implementation. Policy will drive behavior,” he added. Rajeev Singh, Partner, Ernst & Young, said that Omanisation should not be all about the number of jobs provided to Omanis, but in a broader sense there should be more focus on improving the skillsets of existing employees.
