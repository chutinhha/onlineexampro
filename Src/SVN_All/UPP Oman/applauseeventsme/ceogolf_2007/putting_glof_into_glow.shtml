‘Putting’ Glow into golf

OER CEO Golf truly lived up to its reputation – unmatched décor, unique informal gathering of the distinguished and the powerful, and above all, new to Oman – glow golf, taking this premier annual networking event to new heights

It was a day memories are made of. OER CEO Golf 2006 not only put the glitz into golf, but also managed to grip Muscat with golfing fever. Branded as “the biggest networking event” of the country, March 23 saw the coming together of the crème de la crème of Oman’s top business leaders in relaxed surroundings for some fun on the fairways.

Chief executive officers, general managers, directors and other top management came, saw and putted. The culmination of months’ worth of preparation and anticipation, the day was designed to be as serious, as much fun, and as much of a networking opportunity as each participant decided to make it.
Everybody who is anybody in the Sultanate’s corporate world had managed to keep the day free and was looking forward to hitting a few balls around with friends. Amidst a lot of fun and cheering, the branded logo of the event, suspended from a cloud of balloons, was released into the blue skies above Muscat signaling the event inauguration. Then, the 200-plus strong crowd - wearing branded T-shirts and golfing caps – watched as His Highness Sayyid Tarik bin Shabib, editor-in-chief of Oman Economic Review (OER); His Excellency Dr. Mohammad bin Hamad Al Rumhy, Minister of Oil and Gas and; Dr. Amer Rawas, Managing Director of Oman Mobile and tee-off to officially open the day’s events.

Shotgun Start

Appropriately, the shotgun start forewent guns and fireworks, but used the horn of the impressive black Dodge Durango – up for grabs for the first player who managed to get a hole-in-one – as the starting sound for the day’s championship round of golf. Seasoned and experienced golfers, some 60 of them, set off in groups of four to play for the trophies.

Holes 10 to 18 of the Ghallah Wentworth Golf Club, stretching over a total of 3,037 metres, made up the 9-hole challenge that was scored using the Stableford system, taking three handicap categories into account. This was the ‘serious’ part of the day, with several sought-after prizes to be won. Not only could the overall nett champion take home a Rolex Oyster Perpetual watch, but many other prestigious prizes were up for grabs as well. The atmosphere was buzzing with enthusiastic and friendly competition. To add to the excitement, a golf quiz was conducted offering one year membership to the Muscat Golf and Country Club sponsored by Muscat Golf Course Project.

Although golf is the most popular leisure and networking activity of the business elite, not everybody has had a chance to get into the game yet. After all, being the captain of a successful business usually does not leave much leisure time. So, to cater for those who wanted to learn a little more about golf, OER set up a golf clinic to run at the same time as the serious round of golf was in progress.

Cool Clinic

Brandon de Souza, Managing Director of Tiger Sports Marketing (TSM) and a professional golfer himself, and Digraj Singh, Director of TSM, top amateur golfer in the 1970s and former member of a gold-medal-winning team at the SAARC golf championship, imparted lessons on the most important techniques to over 100 golf-beginners. Lessons on swing basics and long game kept them busy for a while.

After the coaching sessions, the participants were grouped into 25 teams of four players, each player of each team specializing in one of the four newly acquired skills. There was the skills competition, with prizes for first, second and third team in the points scoring, as well as individual prizes for the winner of each category of skill.

Both the championship round of golf and the golf clinic with its competition finished in the late afternoon, giving the participants time to relax and chat before getting ready for the quiz, which tested the golfers’ knowledge about the game and brought the lucky winner a one-year free membership to the Muscat Golf and Country Club.

Night Spectacle

Then, upon nightfall, it was time for the most eagerly awaited event of the day: Glow Golf. The scene was spectacularly set: Chinese lanterns lit up the fairways between holes 15 and 18 for the experienced golfers and the putting green by the driving range for the novices. The individual pins, or flags, of each hole were aglow, and even the cups into which the equally glowing golf balls were to be hit, were lit up.

Lasers illuminated the night sky and the golfers and their spouses were adorned with phosphorescent necklaces, making for an eerie as well as spectacular set up. It was a chance to participate in a unique event never seen before in Oman. The ever-lasting memories were formed in a very special and grand atmosphere that had every participant thrilled to have been part of the day.

Sheikh Essa bin Mohammed Al Zedjali, chairman and editor-in-chief of Times of Oman and Al Shabiba, teed-off for the start of the glow-golf and all participants had a great time, enjoying a few swings and shots of golf with a difference. After the glow golf, dinner, live-entertainment, and the prize-giving ceremony were the next eagerly awaited items on the full itinerary, especially considering the amazing prizes that were up for grabs.

At the end of the day, a relaxed crowd watched as Dr. Rawas, Managing Director of Oman Mobile, presented the OER CEO Golf 2006 Champion’s Trophy to the nett individual winner of the day, Fudli Talyar Khan, managing partner, Al Kawther United Establishment. Anil Khimji of Khimji Watches gifted the Rolex Oyster Perpetual watch to the champion. The gross individual winner was Sachin Bawa, CEO, Shanfari Group of Companies, who also took home the OER CEO Golf 2006 Champion’s Trophy as well as a prize.
During the presentations, the special collector’s edition of OER’s TEE TIME was launched and exhibited by HH Sayyid Tarik bin Shabib. Sandeep Sehgal, General Manager of United Media Services, said of the event: “In view of the wonderful response to the event, I am confident that OER CEO Golf will continue to be a regular, must-attend fixture on the business and social calendar in Oman.”

He added: “For months now, the corporate world was buzzing with anticipation, and I believe that the day was an appropriate climax to the months of preparation of the OER CEO Golf team, and expectations of the corporate community.”
