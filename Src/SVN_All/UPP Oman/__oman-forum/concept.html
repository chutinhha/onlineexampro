OMAN FORUM - 2013 EDITION

The 2013 Edition of OMAN FORUM will be based on the theme ‘Job Creation - The National Agenda’. It will be organized on 6 May (Monday) at Oman Auditorium , Al Bustan Palace Ritz-Carlton. Apart from senior bureaucrats from the relevant ministries , over 400 strong audience representing the senior-and-middle level leadership of private sector companies with a combined market capital of over $30 billion are going to attend OMAN FORUM.

This year’s theme ‘Job Creation - The National Agenda’ has been selected after discussions with the relevant stakeholders of the economy and society. According to Muscat Securities Market (by mid Feb 2013) , the market capital of the listed companies is in excess of $31.4 billion. The Government has committed public expenditure of $30 billion in 2013. And Oman is ranked 8th in Middle East and Africa (MEA) region in terms of FDI projects with 48 such projects (an increase of 41 per cent) , as per Financial Times FDI Report 2012. Despite such positive trends , the business community in Oman is concerned about the challenges related to the business growth!

Their anxiousness is not unfounded. They are all concerned about the need to fulfill the single most important national agenda - Job Creation. Another 50 ,000 young Omanis are going to enter the job market in 2013! How are they going to be absorbed? How the new jobs are going to be created? The entry level salary of Omanis has been raised by almost 50 per cent to RO325. The competitors from overseas markets with no such social liabilities are dumping their products and services at much lower costs in Oman. And top it all , with the recent call for capping the expatriates population to one-third of the total population , atleast 200 ,000 expatriates must leave the country!

With such a backdrop , how do we manage the business survival & growth and job creation simultaneously? If not handled successfully , how is it going to affect Oman’s social development and economic growth in the coming years? OMAN FORUM would address these issues in the form of a live debate involving the relevant stakeholders of the economy and society , and contribute to decision making on critical management and policy issues.
