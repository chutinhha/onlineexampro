OER DEBATE
 


Should banks be allowed to take holidays for more than two days in a row?
Starting this month Oman Economic Review is introducing a new column called ‘Debate’. In July, there were two extended weekends with most establishments remaining closed for nearly three days. We ask Raghavan K Murti and Krishna K Gupta for their opinion
 
 BANKS HAVE MOVED TO A FIVE-DAY WEEKEND. HOW DO YOU FEEL ABOUT IT?

Its a long overdue need of the hour. With 90 per cent localisation, banks need the best quality staff to serve the client well and be viable. One of the reasons, the best of the local staff opt for government jobs is because of the holidays, weekends, working hours etc. Talent acquisition and talent retention is basic to a bank’s viability. The banks’ offer in terms of working conditions has to be comparable to the options existing in the market. Efficient bank staff is a key to good service to customers also.

Five day week is a good move specially keeping Thursday as a full day. This way banks get four days for international banking instead of the earlier three and a half days. Saturday is either a half day or full day off internationally so the weekend is aligned with the international practice. From the benefit point of view, five-day working is better than five and a half day as its results in savings on running cost of offices and staff gets required rest and time to spend with family which results in efficiency at work.
 CAN BANKS BE ALLOWED TO REMAIN CLOSED FOR MORE THAN TWO DAYS AT A STRETCH?

Actually with Internet, phone banking, ATM, credit card options the visit to a bank by customers is not warranted at all. Banks should achieve higher penetration in such service usage to accustom customers. For example, instead of charging extra for using an ATM they should reward clients for using automated services like ATM etc. So long as automated services are functional it really does not matter. Look at INGDirect bank, no one ever gets to see their staff at all and no one is complaining.

Banks are indeed part of essential services and should be responsive to the needs of society and the economy. Banks remaining closed continuously for three days causes huge inconvenience to its customers. It also causes hardship for the staff as they have to put through the transactions that have remained pending due to continuous closer of the banks in one or two days after reopening. Banks should not remain closed for more than two days at a stretch in the interest of both bank and its customers alike.
 WHAT ARE THE NORMS FOR BANKS GLOBALLY WHEN THERE ARE THREE OR MORE HOLIDAYS IN A ROW?

People have to take care of themselves. If ATMs are not working we can blame banks but if one forgets his password number how can anyone help. Probably the banks can keep one office open to attend to emergency services without transacting normal businesses like a help desk till the people get used to automated services. This office could be open 24 hrs, 365 days holidays, no holidays.

The central banks of various countries around the world ensure that banking services are made available to its customers even during holidays like this and instructs the banks to remain open. The banks though declare optional holidays internally but keep the branches open. Those who work on such optional holidays are given compensatory offs later.
