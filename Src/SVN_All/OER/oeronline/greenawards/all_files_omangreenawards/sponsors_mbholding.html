Environmental Protection and Conservation

At MB Holding group of companies, we are continuously working towards the protection and conservation of the environment. We develop new projects in an environmentally friendly manner and we ensure that current operations continue to reduce their environmental impact. We have a broad environmental management system in place to drive continuous improvement across our global operations especially in areas such as resource conservation, emissions, discharges and waste.

All staff who have joined our Group of companies are provided with a comprehensive introduction programme to our Health, Safety and Environmental policies, systems and procedures. We strive to minimise the impact of our activities on the environment by meticulous performance management and detailed action plans that are carried out to ensure that the projects adhere to environmental laws and is environmentally acceptable to all stakeholders.

We also promote open communication with stakeholders within the local communities, and promote environmental awareness through sponsoring and participating of environmental awareness events and campaigns within the local communities.

Within the MB Holding Group of companies, we have many examples of environmental protection and conservation promotion projects. These include:

  • Going Greener with a New Gas Project
    While the whole world, particularly the oil and gas sector, is taking measures to cut down costs, Daleel Petroleum has gone ahead to invest in building-up relevant infrastructure to demonstrate its commitment towards environmental protection and conservation. In May 2008, Daleel Petroleum commissioned a gas plant to extract liquids from associated gas that was until then flared. The project has a number of direct and indirect benefits both to the environment and the people of Oman.

     

  • Water Treatment & Recycling
    Daleel Petroleum’s management took a very positive step to minimise impact of its operations to the environment by constructing additional evaporation ponds for storing production water at the main gathering station. In addition, a water treatment plant was commissioned by Daleel’s Engineering Department. This has contributed significantly towards preventing any impact on the environment by treating the water coming from the production system and recycling it via water injection system.

    In addition, the Engineering Department has constructed a waterline to pump production water from gathering station to Block “B” water treatment plant for recycling; this will be an additional milestone in the company’s spotless track-record of responsible environment management.

     

  • Water Well
    As part of its environment conservation effort, Petrogas LLC which operates the Butabul Concession, Block 7, in western Al Wusta province, a remote area with large sand dunes rising from rocky plains and sabkhas, maintained the Abu Butabul water well used by the local community for watering animals and as a source for drilling water. The operations met all the legal environmental requirements and have up-to-date permits for discharges, waste and hazardous materials from concerned respective authorities.

     

  • Lean Gas Usage & Re-injection
    In the Wadi Aswad concession, Block 5, in Al Dhahira province, Daleel is currently implementing a project to remove hydrocarbon liquids and LPG from the associated gas and then to use the lean gas as fuel and conserving the left-over by re-injecting it back into the reservoir. The project, involving a total expenditure of US$ 35 million is shortly due for completion. Earlier, Daleel Petroleum had replaced its BCF fire fighting system with a foam one to comply with the requirements of the authorities regarding adverse effects of BCF chemicals on the ozone layer.

     

  • Environmental Baseline Survey
    Petrogas Rima undertook an environmental baseline survey of the Rima Satellites Small Fields area of Al Wusta. The 1500 square km area comprises parts of the Jiddat al Harasis, Jiddat al Arkad and Wadi Rawnab.

     

  • Collaboration with authorities
    Mawarid Mining is committed to effective environmental management in all exploration, mining and processing activities. It works closely with the Ministry of Environment and Climate Affairs to develop greater environmental awareness and promote environmental protection and preservation of the natural habitats.

     

  • Environmental Monitoring
    Mawarid Mining also carried out full baseline environmental monitoring around its Hatta and Shinas Mine sites and its tailings dam facility. Comprehensive monitoring is carried out at all sites on a regular monthly basis to ensure potential negative impacts are within international (WHO) water quality standards. Dust, vibration, noise and gas monitoring is also undertaken to monitor changes and ensure potential negative impacts are within acceptable standards.

     

  • Rehabilitation and Mine Closure Plan
    Mawarid Mining has a detailed blueprint in the form of Rehabilitation and Mine Closure Plan that is aimed at minimising the footprint of mining activities, restore the natural habitat and ensure conservation of wildlife and natural heritage.

     

  • Mawarid Mining Tailings Dam
    The Tailings Storage facility contains all the relict material (Tailings) after the copper has been extracted from the ore by the Lasail copper concentrator. Mawarid then recovers the process water for reuse in the concentrator, which reduces overall water consumption.

    The tailings storage facility has been lined with a 2 mm HDPE liner to prevent seepage of process water into the ground. A drainage system on top of the liner provides fast extraction of moisture that assists recovery of water and eventual drying of the tailings. An underlined drainage system is installed as a backup system to ensure that no water drains into the ground.

    Moisture sensors and pizieometers are installed in the dam wall to ensure the integrity of the structure. The dam is fenced to protect against incursion of animals and the area is secured from the public. Total holding capacity of the tailing dam is approximately 3 million cubic meters.

     

  • Environmental Day Activities
    Mawarid Mining in coordination with National Training Institute (NTI) sponsored the Environment day (Climate Change) Exhibition in Sohar on June 1st 2008 in order to support and promote environmental protection and conservation. Two schools, Sohar International School and Al-Batinah International School participated. Various activities such as story telling, planting, contests, workshops and drawing were conducted for the children.
