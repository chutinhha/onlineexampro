Clinton Climate Initiative
Carrier partners with companies to support both sustainability and business goals. Carrier signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the Clinton Climate Initiative (CCI) to supply energy-efficient, environmentally sound chillers to the C40 and their private building owners.

The C40 is a group of the world’s largest cities committed to tackling climate change. The C40 cities, which comprise nearly half of the global population, consume 75 percent of the world's energy, and produce nearly 80 percent of its greenhouse gas emissions. We are proud to be the first heating, ventilation, and air-conditioning company to sign an MOU with the Clinton team for energy-efficient products. The MOU results in discounts on these products to C40 cities and other CCI partners.

Energy Modeling and Carbon Footprint Analysis
Carrier has been implementing and sustaining successful energy solutions with our long-term client relationships. Carbon reduction is an integral part of the energy solution. Carrier's carbon reduction method uses a holistic systems engineering approach to model facilities, starting with our Hourly Analysis Program (HAP) with wizard technology. This powerful tool combines comprehensive load estimating, system design, energy simulation and operating cost analysis. The result is an accurate simulation of the interactive effect of the proposed system and equipment changes.

After modeling a facility, each system is analyzed to identify and quantify the carbon equivalents entering and leaving the facility. Carrier experts identify opportunities to improve system efficiencies and suggest alternate energy sources to reduce carbon equivalents. The next step is to equate activities within the building to carbon equivalents in order to evaluate opportunities for improved processes and behaviors, such as waste stream reductions, recycling, and consumable material choices.
Typically, systems with the highest energy consumption are targeted as the best opportunity for carbon reductions. Carbon reduction measures are then identified and annual carbon equivalent reductions determined.

For facilities seeking LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) qualification or just aiming to achieve carbon neutrality, Carrier is ready to deliver the products, technology, expertise and ongoing service that distinguish Carrier as a world leader in high performance building solutions.
